I would like to provide a brief overview of the work opportunities available for individuals interested in joining us for the upcoming blueberry harvest season and beyond. Our harvest typically begins in early July, and we are currently in the preparation phase.

In the weeks leading up to the harvest, we focus on preparing the farm, which includes mowing the grass between the blueberry rows and around each plant. We use specialized machinery for this work and provide training to ensure all team members can operate the equipment safely and effectively. General farm maintenance is also ongoing and includes tasks such as pressure washing, painting, and garbage removal.

Once the blueberries are ripe, harvesting begins. This is done entirely by hand to preserve the quality of the fruit and protect the plants. After picking, the blueberries are weighed in the field, and the total weight is recorded for each harvester. The harvested berries are then transported by tractor to the front of the farm, where they are processed using our blueberry sorting machine. This machine includes a hopper, fan, and conveyor belt. A team of 2–4 people visually inspects and sorts the berries by hand to remove any that are green, soft, or otherwise unsuitable before packaging.

The sorted blueberries are then weighed and packaged at our farm storefront, where customers come to pick up their orders, fresh or frozen, along with other healthy offerings. Additional tasks during the season include cleaning harvesting supplies, such as buckets and flats.

As we continue to grow our farming operation, we are also seeking support in technical and creative areas, including website development, social media management, and marketing.
